4. Shave Right

Translating to, “Don’t shave against the hair growth!” Most shave against the direction of the hair growth as this gives a smoother shave. But in reality, you are nicking the buried portion of the hair follicle, which can lead to inflammation.

Instead, always shave on the direction of your hair growth. Also, don’t go over and over the same area, as it can strip off the upper layer of the skin, leading to inflammation and overexposure. Just do one stroke on each section of the face to avoid this.

And while shaving, lather up with water. Shaving dry can increase the chances of getting cuts, acne, and ingrown hair. Make sure you use water to lather up completely before using the razor.

It also makes it easier to shave, as the hair and the skin get softer. Let the shaving gel or cream sit on your skin once you have lathered it up, and then shave. Your skin will thank you.
